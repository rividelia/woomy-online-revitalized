Woomy-Arras.io

Woomy Arras is a project that started back in 2017-18 and has been worked on by many hands. Throughout most of it's time, it has remained a passion project, being a place where developers can learn to program and put their artistic ideas into the game for others to experience. Given the structure of the team over the years (that being a team where everyone has relatively the same amount of authority), the history is anything but smooth. The game has undergone multiple deletions/abandonments (each with it's own reasons) that have permanently impacted both the game and the community. One of the most notable things is the codebase. Over the years, things have piled ontop of dubious code to "just make things work". This can be largely attributed to three things: this is a passion project, this is where people learned to program, and for most of it's time, it wasnt very organized. This has the end result of the codebase being one of the largest most complex projects that I have ever seen which makes it very difficult to work on and clean up. In recent times, I've (Ayden Greenan/Drkao Hyena) dedicated myself to improving the game and preserving it for any who still enjoy it, and those who wish to mod it. In that time, I've completely redesigned the game to rely off the player's device rather than a centeral server. This was an extremely labor intensive process of rewritting and optimizing absolutely massive parts of the codebase while trying to organize it. Occasionally, Ill release content updates which aim to increase modders' capabilities and keep the game interesting.

The role that the previous developers have played is invaluable and should never be forgotten. Their time and passion, which they have poured into this game, has slowly shaped it into what it is today. Think of it as carving a statue from a block of marble. Which is to say, it has been an extremely long and laborious process filled with ingenuity and creativity. I have the utmost respect for all of those who have contributed to this game and take no credit for their contributions. Personally, I thank all of the previous developers for their work.
